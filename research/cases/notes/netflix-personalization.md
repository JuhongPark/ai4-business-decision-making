## Business Problem

Netflix must help users find relevant content quickly within a very large catalog. Without strong recommendation and discovery systems, search costs increase and user engagement can fall.

## AI Application

Netflix uses recommendation systems that rely on interaction history, title metadata, and contextual viewing signals. More recent work shows the company moving toward a more unified foundation-model approach for personalized recommendation.

## Decision Supported

The system supports content ranking and presentation decisions about what titles to show, in what order, and with what degree of personalization.

## Reported Outcomes

Netflix presents recommendation as central to helping members find content efficiently and to making the product experience more responsive to user taste and context. The 2025 TV experience update also indicates that recommendation quality is tied directly to product design and member discovery.

## Risks or Limitations

Recommendation quality can become costly to maintain at scale. Netflix also notes challenges related to latency, model fragmentation, and the need to capture long-term satisfaction rather than short-term clicks.

## Relevance to Framework

This case is useful because it shows a relatively mature, data-rich decision environment where AI can be deeply embedded in routine business decisions. It is a strong example of AI value in a medium-oversight domain.
