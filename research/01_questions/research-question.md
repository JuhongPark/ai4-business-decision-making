# Research Question

recommended_research_question: Under what conditions should firms use AI to assist, recommend, or automate high-stakes business decisions?
question_status: narrowed after phase 8 expansion review

## Previous Broad Question

previous_question: What factors determine whether AI-supported business decisions lead to better organizational performance?

## Supporting Questions

1. How should firms distinguish between assist, recommend, and automate modes in high-stakes decisions?
2. What governance and oversight conditions are required before AI can influence finance or hiring decisions?
3. How does evidence quality affect trust in reported AI business outcomes?
4. What can lower-risk operational domains reveal by contrast about safe AI deployment?
5. When should firms restrict AI use rather than expand it?

## Working Argument

AI is most defensibly useful in high-stakes business decisions when it augments human judgment, operates with strong evidence and governance, and only moves toward recommendation or automation when the oversight structure is explicit and proportionate to decision risk.

## Scope Anchor

This question will be examined at the firm level with priority attention to finance and hiring as high-stakes domains, plus one lower-risk comparison domain such as operations optimization. AI will be treated as a decision-support capability whose appropriate autonomy level depends on risk, evidence quality, and governance readiness.
