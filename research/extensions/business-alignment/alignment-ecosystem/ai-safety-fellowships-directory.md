## Classification Method

All 130 entries were classified with a keyword-matching pass. "AI-related" refers to titles or descriptions containing terms such as AI, artificial intelligence, alignment, safety, machine learning, ML, interpretability, or LLM. "Direct AI safety/alignment/governance" applies a stricter keyword filter including ai safety, ai risk, interpretability, ai governance, ai policy, ai agent, and catastrophic ai. Because the filter is mechanical, it likely misses programs that use adjacent terms such as responsible AI or trustworthy AI.

## Key Analysis

1. **Policy skill prevalence:** 77 of 130 programs list policy as a required skill. This may reflect the field's emphasis on policy-literate talent, though it could also reflect funding preferences or the fact that policy fellowships are more numerous in general (not specific to AI).
2. **Entry-level concentration:** 62 programs target entry-level. This may reflect the field's emphasis on pipeline-building, though it could also reflect the dataset's bias toward academic and early-career opportunities rather than mid-career programs that may exist outside this listing.
3. **US geographic concentration:** 82 programs are US-based (63%), predominantly Washington DC. UK is a distant second with 17.
4. **Most programs are closed:** Only 28 of 130 are currently accessible (20 rolling + 8 open). Timing matters — most cycles are Jan–Mar for summer cohorts.
5. **Direct AI safety appears niche by this measure:** Only 19 of 130 programs matched the stricter keyword filter for AI safety/alignment. This count is an artifact of the mechanical filter and likely undercounts programs with safety-relevant content that use different terminology (e.g., "responsible AI," "trustworthy AI").

## What This Dataset Suggests

1. **Geographic concentration:** AI governance fellowships cluster in Washington DC, London, Oxford, and the SF Bay Area. Outside those hubs, the clearest options are the 35 Remote/Global entries.
2. **Timing matters:** Most programs close on a yearly cycle between January and March. Rolling programs remain the most flexible entry point.
3. **Experience range is broad:** The dataset spans entry-level through senior roles. Governance-focused tracks such as IAPS, GovAI, and Talos are accessible without a strong technical background.
4. **Stipends vary widely:** Compensation ranges from unpaid participation through high-stipend fellowships such as IAPS and Tarbell.
5. **Technical vs policy split:** Technical safety tracks such as MATS, Anthropic, and LASR generally require ML capability, while governance tracks more often welcome non-technical applicants.
6. **Direct AI safety looks niche under a strict filter:** Only 19 of 130 entries match the narrow AI safety keyword pass, but that is likely an undercount because many adjacent programs use different terminology.

## Notes

- The 80,000 Hours list is curated around the EA network, so opportunities outside that network are underrepresented.
- The keyword classification is mechanical and may misclassify boundary cases.
