# Case Analysis Draft

The case analysis suggests that AI is easiest to justify in structured and lower-risk environments, but becomes more difficult to justify as decision stakes and fairness risks increase. UPS illustrates favorable conditions for AI-supported operational decisions. Route optimization is repeated, measurable, and supported by strong operational data. However, the reported benefits are primarily company-reported and therefore are more useful as design evidence than as independently verified proof of performance.

By contrast, Upstart and the Reuters-reported Amazon recruiting case suggest that high-impact decisions require more than predictive capability. Upstart demonstrates how AI can expand analytical capacity in lending, but it also shows how governance pressure increases when AI influences consequential financial outcomes. Fairness, compliance, and model change risk become central design concerns, and regulator-backed documentation gives this case a stronger evidentiary basis than the others. The Amazon recruiting case functions as a warning example because historical bias can be encoded into AI-supported screening while the evidence remains weaker and less directly auditable.

Across the cases, the same underlying capabilities of prediction, ranking, and optimization produce different implications depending on data quality, objective design, oversight structure, domain sensitivity, and evidence strength. This supports the argument that the choice among assist, recommend, and automate should be risk-sensitive rather than uniform.
