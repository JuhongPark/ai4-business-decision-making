## Summary

This EEOC technical assistance document clarifies that Title VII applies when employers use software, algorithms, and AI to make or inform selection decisions. It emphasizes disparate impact analysis and makes clear that employers remain responsible for the effects of automated selection tools.

## Key Arguments

- AI and automated selection tools in hiring are subject to existing anti-discrimination law.
- Employers should assess whether such tools have adverse impact on protected groups.
- Compliance responsibility is not transferred away from the employer simply because a vendor or technical system is involved.

## Evidence

The document provides regulatory interpretation under Title VII and explains how employers should evaluate adverse impact when automated systems are used in employment selection procedures.

## Relevance to Research Question

This source materially strengthens the hiring side of the study. It supports the claim that high-stakes HR decisions require strong oversight, evidence, and restriction logic before AI is allowed to influence outcomes.

## Useful Quotes

- Employers remain responsible for ensuring that AI-informed selection decisions comply with anti-discrimination law.
