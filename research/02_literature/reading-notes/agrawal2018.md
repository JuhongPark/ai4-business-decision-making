# Reading Note

source_id: agrawal2018
title: Prediction, Judgment and Complexity: A Theory of Decision Making and Artificial Intelligence
author: Ajay K. Agrawal, Joshua S. Gans, Avi Goldfarb
year: 2018
type: working paper
link: https://www.nber.org/papers/w24243

## Summary

This paper frames AI as an improvement in prediction technology and explains how better prediction changes decision-making under uncertainty. It distinguishes prediction from judgment and argues that the relationship between the two depends on environmental complexity and the possibility of automation.

## Key Arguments

- AI lowers the cost of prediction.
- Prediction and judgment are complements in many decision settings.
- In complex environments, the value of judgment remains high unless the decision can be highly automated.

## Evidence

The paper develops a theoretical model of decision-making under uncertainty and uses it to explain how AI affects risky choice, complexity, contracts, and firm boundaries.

## Relevance to Research Question

This is the strongest conceptual source for explaining why AI does not automatically replace managers in business decision-making. It directly supports the study's focus on conditions rather than generic capability claims.

## Useful Quotes

- AI should be treated as a prediction technology rather than a general replacement for judgment.

## My Notes

This source should anchor the conceptual section of the literature review. It gives the research a clear way to separate prediction quality from actual decision quality.
