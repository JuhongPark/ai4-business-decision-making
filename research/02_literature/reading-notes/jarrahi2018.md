link: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bushor.2018.03.007

## Summary

This article argues against a simple human-versus-machine framing and instead presents AI and humans as complementary contributors to organizational decision-making. It emphasizes the continued value of human intuition and contextual reasoning.

## Key Arguments

- AI is effective in analytical processing and pattern recognition.
- Humans remain important in uncertainty, ambiguity, and equivocal settings.
- Organizational value comes from human-AI symbiosis rather than simple substitution.

## Evidence

The article synthesizes conceptual and practical arguments about organizational decision processes, highlighting differences between analytical and intuitive decision modes.

## Relevance to Research Question

This source directly supports the study's working argument that AI creates stronger business value when it augments human judgment.

## Useful Quotes

- Human-AI symbiosis provides a better frame than replacement when studying organizational decision-making.

## My Notes

This should be used in both the literature review and the final framework section. It is especially useful for explaining why oversight is not only a control mechanism but also a source of better decisions.
