## Summary

The NIST AI RMF provides a practical framework for identifying and managing AI risks while promoting trustworthy and responsible AI use. It emphasizes governance, measurement, and ongoing management rather than one-time compliance.

## Key Arguments

- AI risk management should be continuous and organizationally embedded.
- Trustworthy AI requires governance, mapping, measurement, and management.
- AI deployment should be adapted to context, use case, and risk profile.

## Evidence

The framework is designed as an operational resource for organizations that design, develop, deploy, or use AI systems across sectors.

## Relevance to Research Question

This source supports the study's claim that governance is a determinant of decision quality, not only a compliance consideration.
