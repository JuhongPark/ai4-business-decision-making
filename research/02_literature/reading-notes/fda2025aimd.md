## Summary

This FDA resource frames AI in medical devices around safety, effectiveness, transparency, lifecycle management, and regulatory submission expectations. It makes clear that healthcare AI is not only a capability issue but also a validation and oversight issue.

## Key Arguments

- AI-enabled medical devices must be evaluated for safety and effectiveness.
- Lifecycle management and transparency matter because AI systems can be iterative and data-driven.
- Healthcare AI decisions require stronger regulatory and evidentiary discipline than ordinary business workflows.

## Evidence

The FDA page describes current guidance direction, real-world device applications, and related regulatory expectations for AI-enabled medical devices.

## Relevance to Research Question

This source supports the study's conclusion that healthcare is a high-upside but high-risk domain where assist or tightly governed recommend is more defensible than broad automation.

## Useful Quotes

- Healthcare AI should be governed through safety, effectiveness, and lifecycle management expectations.
