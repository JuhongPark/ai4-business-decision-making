# Reading Note

source_id: dietvorst2015
title: Algorithm Aversion: People Erroneously Avoid Algorithms After Seeing Them Err
author: Berkeley J. Dietvorst, Joseph P. Simmons, Cade Massey
year: 2015
type: journal article
link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25401381/

## Summary

This article shows that people often reject algorithmic advice after observing errors, even when the algorithm performs better than human forecasters. It identifies a behavioral barrier to effective use of decision-support tools.

## Key Arguments

- People can become less willing to use algorithms after seeing them fail.
- Human decision makers may compare algorithmic mistakes more harshly than human mistakes.
- Adoption quality depends partly on managerial trust behavior.

## Evidence

The study reports experimental evidence showing that participants prefer human forecasting after seeing algorithmic error, even when the algorithm remains superior on average.

## Relevance to Research Question

This source adds a behavioral dimension to the study. It explains why technically useful AI systems may still fail to improve decision quality inside organizations.

## Useful Quotes

- Decision quality can deteriorate when managers abandon better algorithmic tools because of observed error.

## My Notes

This source should be paired with discussion of overreliance later so the study does not treat trust only as a deficit problem.
