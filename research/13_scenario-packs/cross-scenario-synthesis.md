# Phase 13: Cross-Scenario Synthesis

status: completed
domains: operations, finance, healthcare
comparison_basis: baseline, stress, edge-case conditions across AI role modes

## Core Pattern

The preferred role of AI is not fixed within a domain. It shifts with scenario conditions.

## Shared Findings

### Finding 1: Baseline conditions tolerate more AI autonomy than stress or edge cases

Operations can justify bounded automation in stable environments. Finance and healthcare can sometimes justify stronger recommendation support in baseline conditions, but not broad automation.

### Finding 2: Stress conditions increase the value of AI support but also increase the need for human oversight

In all three domains, AI becomes more useful under pressure because speed and consistency matter more. However, stress conditions also magnify the cost of hidden model failure.

### Finding 3: Edge cases reverse the autonomy logic

When the environment becomes uncertain, incomplete, or out-of-distribution, the study's preferred mode shifts back toward assist rather than recommend or automate.

### Finding 4: Domain type still matters

- operations is the strongest automation candidate
- finance is a restriction-heavy recommendation domain
- healthcare is a safety-first recommendation domain

## Strategic Interpretation

The central lesson is that AI autonomy should be scenario-sensitive rather than domain-static. Even in a domain that is generally automation-friendly, edge cases can justify fallback to human-led decision-making.

## Recommendation for the Report

The think-tank report should treat scenario variation as a core design principle, not as a secondary appendix. This is where the strongest analytical contribution now sits.
