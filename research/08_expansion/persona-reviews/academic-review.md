# Expansion Plan Review

reviewer: Dr. Elena Park
target: research/08_expansion/phase-08-expansion-plan.md
overall_assessment: strong_direction_needs_tighter_research_design

## Strengths

- The plan correctly identifies that the current study is stronger as a framework paper than as a causal performance claim.
- The recommended shift toward human-AI collaboration under varying risk conditions is academically coherent.
- The evidence upgrade path is realistic and methodologically useful.

## Concerns

### Concern 1: The narrowed topic is promising but still broad

Human-AI collaboration under different risk conditions is stronger than the current topic, but it may still be too wide if it covers all business functions at once. The next revision should decide whether the study remains cross-functional or concentrates on a limited set of high-contrast domains.

### Concern 2: The methodology upgrade needs clearer design language

The plan should specify what counts as a structured comparative case study. It needs explicit unit of analysis, case selection logic, evidence hierarchy, and evaluation dimensions.

### Concern 3: Contribution statement should be more precise

The expansion plan should say whether the next study aims to contribute to theory, managerial decision design, governance practice, or all three. If it aims at all three, the tradeoff should be acknowledged.

## Recommendations

1. Narrow the study to two or three decision domains instead of all five
2. Define the comparative case method in a more formal way
3. State the intended contribution hierarchy more explicitly
