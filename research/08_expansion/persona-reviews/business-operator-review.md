# Expansion Plan Review

reviewer: Marcus Hale
target: research/08_expansion/phase-08-expansion-plan.md
overall_assessment: strong_and_practical_with_better_output_prioritization

## Strengths

- The plan moves in the right direction by protecting the useful parts of the current study instead of discarding them.
- The focus on assist, recommend, and automate is highly usable for managers.
- The output strategy of paper plus executive summary plus one-page framework is commercially and organizationally practical.

## Concerns

### Concern 1: The managerial audience should be prioritized more clearly

The plan still sounds academically centered. If the goal is practical influence, the manager-facing deliverables should not be treated as secondary packaging. They should be designed alongside the revised paper.

### Concern 2: Cross-functional breadth can dilute practical value

Managers benefit more from clear contrasts than from broad coverage. Comparing a structured domain and a high-stakes domain may be more actionable than keeping a wide map across all business functions.

### Concern 3: Evidence grading should be made visible in outputs

If the research upgrades its evidence quality, managers should see that directly. A simple evidence-strength label in case tables would add major credibility.

## Recommendations

1. Keep the framework central
2. Limit the next-stage comparison to a smaller number of high-value domains
3. Build evidence labels directly into the case matrix and executive summary
