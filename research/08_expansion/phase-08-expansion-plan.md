# Phase 8: Research Expansion Plan

status: draft_v1
phase_goal: Define how the current research on AI for business decision-making can be expanded into a stronger and more defensible next-stage study.

## Expansion Objective

The current study produced a qualitative framework supported by literature and illustrative cases. The next stage should strengthen the research by improving evidence quality, sharpening the scope, and increasing both academic and practical usefulness.

## Expansion Directions

### Direction 1: Narrow the core question into a higher-value subtopic

The current question is broad enough to support a conceptual study, but a stronger next-stage paper should narrow its focus. Three promising directions are:

1. AI in high-stakes business decisions: finance, hiring, and compliance-sensitive domains
2. Human-AI collaboration in managerial decision-making: when should AI assist, recommend, or automate
3. Governance as a performance condition: how oversight affects AI decision quality in firms

## Recommended Priority

The strongest next-stage topic is:

recommended_topic: Human-AI collaboration in business decision-making under different risk conditions

This direction preserves the current framework, fits the existing evidence base, and creates a stronger bridge between theory, managerial practice, and governance.

### Direction 2: Upgrade the methodology

The current study is a qualitative synthesis based on public sources. A stronger version could use one or more of the following upgrades:

1. Add a formal comparative case design with common evidence criteria
2. Build a structured evaluation matrix across industries and decision types
3. Add a small expert interview component if access becomes possible
4. Separate primary, secondary, and company-reported evidence more systematically

## Recommended Method Upgrade

recommended_method_upgrade: structured comparative case study with explicit evidence grading

This is the most realistic next step because it improves rigor without requiring immediate access to proprietary firm data.

### Direction 3: Improve the evidence base

The research should move from illustrative cases toward stronger evidence classification. Future work should:

1. Prefer regulator documents, annual reports, earnings materials, and technical disclosures over broad summaries
2. Use investigative reporting only when primary sources are unavailable and label it clearly
3. Track whether claims are company-reported, independently reported, regulator-supported, or academically supported
4. Distinguish outcome claims from design claims

## Evidence Upgrade Goal

The next-stage study should include a visible evidence-strength layer so readers can see which claims are strongly supported and which are more tentative.

### Direction 4: Expand the output formats

The current work is a draft paper. It can be expanded into multiple output types:

1. an academic-style paper
2. a managerial briefing
3. a decision framework playbook
4. a presentation deck

## Recommended Output Strategy

recommended_outputs:
- research paper revision
- executive summary for managers
- one-page framework sheet

This creates both academic and practical value without splitting the project too early.

## Next-Stage Research Questions

1. Under what conditions should firms rely on AI to assist, recommend, or automate decisions?
2. How do oversight and governance affect AI performance in high-stakes business domains?
3. Which business functions show the strongest fit for AI-supported decisions, and why?
4. How should firms evaluate evidence before trusting AI-driven business outcomes?

## Planned Work Packages

### Work Package 1: Scope Refinement

- choose one narrowed core topic
- rewrite the main research question
- align chapter structure with the narrower argument

### Work Package 2: Evidence Upgrade

- classify current sources by evidence strength
- replace weak case support where possible
- add stronger primary or regulator-backed sources

### Work Package 3: Case Design Improvement

- define fixed comparison criteria
- rebuild the case table with evidence labels
- separate positive, mixed, and cautionary cases

### Work Package 4: Framework Revision

- make the framework more explicit for high-risk versus low-risk domains
- connect adoption modes more directly to governance requirements
- clarify practical decision rules for managers

### Work Package 5: Output Revision

- revise the paper around the narrower question
- prepare a manager-friendly summary
- prepare a concise final recommendation sheet

## Suggested Execution Order

1. Narrow the topic
2. Upgrade the evidence base
3. Rebuild the case comparison
4. Revise the framework
5. Rewrite the paper and secondary outputs

## Immediate Recommendation

The best next move is not to add more broad content. The best next move is to tighten the study around human-AI collaboration under different risk levels and strengthen the evidence quality of the existing case structure.
