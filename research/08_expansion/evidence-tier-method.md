# Evidence Tier Method

status: active
purpose: Classify evidence strength for the next-stage study on human-AI collaboration in high-stakes business decision-making.

## Evidence Tiers

### Tier 1

Highest-confidence evidence used for consequential claims.

- regulator documents
- court records
- audited reports
- peer-reviewed journal articles
- formal technical disclosures with direct methodological detail

## Use Rule

Use Tier 1 sources for:

- claims about high-stakes outcomes
- fairness, compliance, or accountability claims
- claims about measured organizational impact in sensitive domains

### Tier 2

Useful but less independently verified evidence.

- annual reports
- investor materials
- official company technical blogs
- executive statements
- official product or policy documentation

## Use Rule

Use Tier 2 sources for:

- design descriptions
- operating logic
- company-stated goals or reported benefits

Tier 2 evidence should not be treated as equivalent to independent proof of impact.

### Tier 3

Contextual or fallback evidence with weaker direct verification.

- investigative journalism
- credible trade press
- secondary summaries

## Use Rule

Use Tier 3 sources only when:

- primary evidence is unavailable
- the claim is clearly labeled as reported rather than fully verified
- the source is not used alone to support strong causal claims

## Claim Types

### Design Claims

Statements about how a system was intended to work, where it was deployed, and what role it played in workflow design.

### Outcome Claims

Statements about performance improvement, fairness outcomes, efficiency gains, or business impact.

## Interpretation Rule

Outcome claims require stronger evidence than design claims. A Tier 2 source may be sufficient for a design claim, but high-impact outcome claims should rely primarily on Tier 1 support.
