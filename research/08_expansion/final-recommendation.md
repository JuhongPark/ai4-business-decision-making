# Final Expansion Recommendation

status: completed
basis: phase 8 draft plan plus three persona reviews

## Final Assessment

The expansion plan is directionally correct, but it should become narrower, more explicit, and more rigorous before further drafting begins. The current research is strongest as a framework-centered qualitative study. Its next evolution should therefore strengthen depth and evidence quality rather than expand breadth.

## Recommended Strategic Choice

recommended_next_stage_topic: Human-AI collaboration in high-stakes business decision-making

## Why This Topic Wins

1. It preserves the strongest part of the current study, which is the framework on assist, recommend, and automate with guardrails.
2. It creates a stronger research contribution than a general business-AI overview.
3. It allows governance, oversight, and evidence quality to remain central rather than peripheral.
4. It makes the study more defensible because high-stakes domains naturally justify caution, comparison, and structured evaluation.

## Recommended Scope Reduction

The next-stage revision should not continue to treat all business functions equally. It should focus on:

- finance and credit-related decisions
- hiring or workforce screening decisions
- one lower-risk comparison domain such as marketing personalization or operations optimization

This design preserves cross-domain contrast without sacrificing analytical sharpness.

## Recommended Method Revision

The next study should be defined as:

recommended_method: structured comparative case study with evidence tiers

## Required Method Elements

1. fixed unit of analysis
2. explicit case selection criteria
3. evidence hierarchy
4. common comparison matrix
5. distinction between design claims and outcome claims

## Required Evidence Tiers

### Tier 1

Regulator documents, court records, audited reports, formal technical disclosures, peer-reviewed research

### Tier 2

Annual reports, investor materials, official company technical blogs, executive statements

### Tier 3

Investigative journalism, credible trade press, secondary summaries

## Rule for Use

High-impact claims should rely primarily on Tier 1 and Tier 2 evidence. Tier 3 should be used only when necessary and should be labeled clearly.

## Framework Revision Priorities

1. add explicit restriction logic for sensitive domains
2. make evidence strength visible inside case tables
3. sharpen default guidance for assist versus recommend versus automate
4. tie governance intensity directly to decision impact

## Output Priorities

### Priority 1

Revise the core research paper around the narrower topic

### Priority 2

Create a manager-facing executive summary that uses the same framework and evidence labels

### Priority 3

Prepare a one-page decision sheet summarizing adoption modes, risk levels, and oversight rules

## Recommended Immediate Next Edits

1. rewrite the research question around high-stakes human-AI collaboration
2. rebuild the case set around two high-stakes domains and one lower-risk comparison case
3. add an evidence-tier system to the case documents
4. revise the framework to include restriction defaults

## Final Position

The next version of the research should become narrower, more explicit about evidence quality, and more strongly centered on high-stakes human-AI decision design. That path offers the best combination of academic defensibility, managerial usefulness, and governance rigor.
