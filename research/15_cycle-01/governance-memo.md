# Cycle 01: Governance Memo

status: completed
purpose: Derive governance implications from the first scenario research cycle.

## Main Governance Lessons

### Lesson 1: Escalation authority must be conditional

Organizations should not assign one permanent AI role to a domain. The role should change when operating conditions change.

### Lesson 2: Fallback design is as important as deployment design

Most AI governance frameworks emphasize launch conditions. This research shows that fallback rules under stress and edge cases are equally important.

### Lesson 3: High-stakes domains require evidence before autonomy

In finance and healthcare, organizations should require stronger evidence and stronger auditability before increasing AI authority.

### Lesson 4: Explainability burden rises under uncertainty

Under baseline conditions, organizations may accept lower explanation depth in repeated workflows. Under stress or edge cases, explanation quality becomes more important because the cost of hidden failure rises.

## Recommended Governance Rules

1. define baseline mode, stress mode, and edge-case mode before deployment
2. define escalation triggers and fallback triggers in advance
3. treat fairness, safety, and subgroup anomalies as autonomy-reducing signals
4. keep human override visible and operational in high-stakes domains
5. separate evidence for system design from evidence for claimed business impact

## Executive Implication

Executives should ask not only whether an AI system performs well, but whether the organization knows when to reduce its authority.
