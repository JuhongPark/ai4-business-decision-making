## Strengths

- The report is now more defensible because it includes domain-specific fallback logic.
- The scenario design reduces the temptation to make broad claims from thin evidence.
- Governance recommendations are more concrete than before.

## Findings

### Finding 1: Fallback triggers should be stated more explicitly in the report

The current report implies them, but the governance case becomes stronger if fallback triggers are listed directly.

### Finding 2: Healthcare automation caution should remain very strong

The report should avoid any ambiguity that surge conditions make broad healthcare automation acceptable. The preferred mode is stronger recommendation support, not full automation.

### Finding 3: Finance stress conditions should explicitly emphasize fairness and drift

These are the right limiting conditions and should be named clearly in the main text.

## Recommendation

Revise the report so fallback triggers and high-stakes restriction rules are explicit, not only implied.
