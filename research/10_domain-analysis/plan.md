# Phase 10: Domain Expansion Plan

status: active
goal: Analyze AI use across eight business fields using a common decision-governance framework.

## Fields in Scope

1. strategy
2. operations
3. finance
4. healthcare
5. investment
6. product
7. marketing
8. market research

## Why a Plan Is Needed First

The field list is broad and mixes regulated, semi-regulated, and low-regulation decision environments. A common comparison method is necessary so the analysis does not collapse into disconnected observations.

## Analysis Method

Each field will be evaluated on the same seven dimensions:

1. typical decision types
2. primary AI role
3. expected business value
4. main risks
5. recommended autonomy mode
6. evidence strength
7. research priority

## Analytical Logic

- regulated and high-stakes domains will be treated more conservatively
- lower-risk domains will be evaluated for controlled automation potential
- evidence strength will affect how confidently each field can be interpreted
- final recommendations will distinguish where AI should assist, recommend, automate with guardrails, or remain restricted

## Expected Output

The analysis will produce:

- one cross-domain comparison table
- one field-by-field interpretation
- one synthesis identifying which domains are best suited for immediate AI use and which require stronger restrictions

## Working Assumption

The broadest value of this phase is not to prove equal AI readiness across all fields. It is to show that AI readiness differs sharply by decision structure, legal exposure, evidence quality, and governance burden.
