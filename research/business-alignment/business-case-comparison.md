# Business Case Comparison: Alignment and Commercial Deployment

status: draft
date: 2026-03-25
purpose: compare current cases that show how alignment and governance appear in actual deployment and commercialization settings

## Why This Note Exists

The repository's new business-alignment phase needs more concrete cases.

This note does not try to prove universal business outcomes.

It compares a small set of current cases to show where governance and alignment matter in practice, what kind of business pathway is visible, and how strong the evidence is.

## Reading Rule

This comparison should be read as mechanism analysis, not as a universal performance ranking.

The main question is:

- what does each case reveal about the relationship between alignment, governance, and deployability?

## Case Notes

### 1. EU AI Act

What it contributes:

- a strong example of governance as a legal gating mechanism
- a clear reason to treat deployability as part of the alignment problem

Interpretation:

If a covered system needs documentation, oversight, risk management, and conformity infrastructure before market placement, governance is part of product viability rather than an optional later-stage activity.

### 2. OMB M-25-22

What it contributes:

- a strong example of trustworthy AI requirements entering acquisition practice
- explicit linkage between AI procurement, cross-functional review, testing, and responsible lifecycle management

Interpretation:

This case shows that governance and alignment artifacts are becoming procurement design variables rather than only internal policy artifacts.

### 3. Microsoft SSPA

What it contributes:

- a concrete enterprise example where AI systems fall inside supplier assurance requirements
- evidence that governance can affect whether work begins at all

Interpretation:

This case is useful because it shows that governance can become part of enterprise relationship formation and not just post-sale oversight.

### 4. Microsoft ISO 42001

What it contributes:

- a major platform vendor using certification to reassure customers about AI governance practices

Interpretation:

The case supports the claim that assurance artifacts are increasingly part of how enterprise AI offerings are legitimized and sold.

### 5. JAGGAER ISO 42001

What it contributes:

- a vendor explicitly treating certification as confidence infrastructure for customers

Interpretation:

This is not clean causal evidence of commercial impact, but it is useful directional evidence that governance artifacts are becoming market-facing signals.

### 6. OneTrust and Credo AI

What they contribute:

- evidence that governance is being productized as operational infrastructure
- strong emphasis on inventories, policy enforcement, monitoring, audit-ready evidence, and scalable approvals

Interpretation:

These cases show movement from governance as paper policy toward governance as software-supported control architecture.

## Cross-Case Pattern

Across these cases, four recurring pathways appear.

### 1. Governance as a gate

Seen most clearly in:

- EU AI Act
- OMB procurement rules
- enterprise supplier assurance

### 2. Governance as acceleration infrastructure

Seen most clearly in:

- governance platforms that standardize intake, ownership, review, and evidence collection

### 3. Governance as trust signal

Seen most clearly in:

- ISO 42001 certification cases
- enterprise-facing product positioning around assurance and responsible AI

### 4. Governance as runtime control

Seen most clearly in:

- platform emphasis on continuous monitoring and policy enforcement for evolving AI systems

## Implications For The Repository

### 1. Business cases should be grouped by value pathway rather than by brand alone

A useful grouping is:

- gating cases
- acceleration cases
- assurance cases
- runtime control cases

### 2. The strongest cases are the ones with institutional consequences

The most credible examples are those where governance affects:

- market entry
- procurement eligibility
- supplier approval
- documented assurance obligations

These are stronger than generic claims that governance is simply "good for business."

### 3. Vendor cases should be used carefully

Vendor pages are useful for showing market framing and the kinds of capabilities buyers are being asked to purchase.

They should not be read as neutral causal proof of realized business performance.
