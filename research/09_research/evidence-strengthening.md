# Phase 9: Evidence Strengthening

status: completed
phase_goal: Strengthen the refocused study with more primary and regulator-backed evidence for high-stakes domains.

## What Was Strengthened

### Finance

The Upstart lending case remains the strongest high-stakes case because it includes CFPB no-action letter materials and a termination order. This gives the study direct regulatory evidence on monitoring, model change, and governance.

### Hiring

The hiring domain was strengthened by adding EEOC technical assistance on AI and adverse impact in employment selection. This improves the legal and governance basis for claims about restriction defaults in hiring, even though the Amazon case itself remains a reported warning case.

### Operations

The UPS ORION case remains a lower-risk comparison case. Its value lies mainly in design logic and company-reported efficiency claims, not in independent verification of business performance.

## Main Research Effect

The study now has a stronger evidentiary basis for its central argument:

- finance supports high-stakes governance claims with regulator-backed evidence
- hiring supports restriction logic with primary legal guidance from EEOC
- operations remains a useful contrast case for controlled automation in lower-risk settings

## Updated Interpretation

The strongest part of the research is no longer simply that AI should be used carefully. The stronger claim is that high-stakes human-AI collaboration should be governed differently depending on the legal, evidentiary, and operational characteristics of the decision domain.
