## Summary

MIT System Design and Management has relevant research for this project, but the pattern differs from MIT Sloan. The strongest SDM evidence appears in student theses, applied systems-thinking work, and linked interdisciplinary programs rather than in a single long-running AI decision-governance lab. Even so, SDM has clearly examined AI commercialization, AI-supported high-stakes investment decisions, AI-supported enterprise evaluation tools, and AI accountability questions.

## Main Findings

- MIT SDM has relevant research, but mostly through thesis and applied-research channels rather than a single dedicated faculty lab focused only on AI decision governance.
- The SDM contribution is strongest where AI is treated as part of a larger sociotechnical system involving workflows, stakeholders, business models, and implementation constraints.
- The SDM venture capital thesis is especially relevant because it applies AI to a genuinely high-stakes business judgment setting rather than only to low-risk operational optimization.
- The commercialization thesis is useful because it focuses on use-case selection, strategic fit, and stakeholder implications, which aligns with this study's emphasis on conditional adoption rather than blanket automation.
- The SERC-linked research is relevant because it pushes SDM's contribution toward accountability, data ownership, and ethical system design rather than pure capability claims.

## Relevance to Research Question

This SDM evidence supports the current study in a different way from Sloan research. Sloan contributes stronger organizational and empirical evidence on performance, labor, and adoption. SDM contributes a systems-design perspective that helps explain how AI authority should be embedded in workflows, infrastructure, and organizational structures. In that sense, SDM research is especially useful for the later implementation-facing parts of this project.

## Caveat

Based on the official SDM materials reviewed, it would be inaccurate to describe MIT SDM as having one dominant research lab devoted specifically to AI authority in high-stakes business decision-making. A better characterization is that SDM has produced a meaningful but distributed body of related research across theses, webinars, and interdisciplinary programs.

## My Notes

This note is best used as a supplement to the MIT-wide research landscape. It shows that MIT SDM is relevant to this project, especially where the research turns from abstract governance logic toward system design, implementation, and interdisciplinary accountability.
