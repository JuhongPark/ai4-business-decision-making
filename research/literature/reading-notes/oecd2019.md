## Summary

The OECD AI Principles provide broad normative guidance for trustworthy AI, including human-centered values, transparency, robustness, security, accountability, and responsible stewardship.

## Key Arguments

- AI systems should support inclusive growth, human rights, and human-centered values.
- Transparency and explainability matter for trustworthy adoption.
- Accountability remains necessary for actors involved in AI systems.

## Evidence

The principles were adopted to guide responsible AI policy and practice across governments and organizations.

## Relevance to Research Question

This source provides a normative foundation for including accountability and human oversight in the study's analytical framework.
