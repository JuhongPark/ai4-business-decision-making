## Strengths

- The framework is understandable and practical.
- The adoption modes of assist, recommend, and automate with guardrails are useful for managerial audiences.
- The paper clearly distinguishes business functions with different oversight needs.

## Findings

### Finding 1: The framework is useful, but managers need stronger caution on self-reported success cases

Netflix, UPS, and Upstart all provide helpful examples, but several outcome claims rely on company or company-adjacent materials. A business reader should be warned that these cases illustrate logic and design conditions more than they prove ROI in a fully comparable way.

### Finding 2: The paper is strongest when it translates evidence into decision criteria

The most practical parts of the draft are the structured comparisons and adoption modes. These should remain central in the final validated interpretation.

### Finding 3: HR and finance need stronger warning language

The paper already treats HR and finance as higher-risk domains, but the final judgment should say more clearly that these domains should default to assist or tightly controlled recommend models unless strong governance evidence exists.

## Required Revisions

1. Emphasize that case examples are illustrative, not directly comparable proof of value
2. Strengthen final recommendations for HR and finance
3. Preserve the framework as the main managerial takeaway

## Verdict

The paper is useful to operators if it is presented as a decision framework supported by indicative cases rather than as a universal proof of AI business success.
