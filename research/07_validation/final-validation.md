# Phase 7: Final Validation

status: completed
phase_goal: Stress-test the research using three independent reviewer personas and produce a final judgment on defensibility.

## Review Set

1. Academic Reviewer
2. Business Operator
3. Risk and Governance Auditor

## Shared Findings Across Reviewers

### Finding 1: The core argument is sound

All three reviewers agree that the study's main claim is coherent: AI value in business decision-making is conditional on decision structure, data quality, human oversight, and governance.

### Finding 2: The framework is the strongest part of the research

The reviewers converge on the view that the practical framework is more defensible than any broad performance claim. This means the final validated paper should be read primarily as a structured qualitative framework supported by literature and illustrative cases.

### Finding 3: Evidence quality is uneven across the cases

The strongest external support appears in the literature and in regulator-backed material such as the CFPB documentation on Upstart. By contrast, Netflix and UPS rely heavily on official company materials, and the Amazon hiring case relies on Reuters-reported information rather than direct corporate disclosure.

### Finding 4: High-stakes domains require more explicit caution

All reviewers agree that finance and human resources should be treated more cautiously than the current broad business framing might suggest. These domains should default to stronger oversight and tighter governance assumptions.

## Revisions Applied After Review

1. Softened causal language in the final paper
2. Added explicit limitations on case evidence quality
3. Labeled company-reported and Reuters-reported evidence more carefully
4. Strengthened caution for HR and finance in the final judgment

## Final Judgment

final_status: validated_with_qualified_confidence
interpretation: The research is defensible as a qualitative, framework-driven study supported by foundational literature and illustrative cases. It should not be presented as a definitive causal proof of AI business performance effects across all settings.

## Use Guidance

- Safe use: conceptual framing, comparative reasoning, managerial evaluation framework
- Cautious use: case-based claims about business outcomes
- Unsafe use: strong causal claims that imply uniform ROI or universal decision improvement from AI adoption
