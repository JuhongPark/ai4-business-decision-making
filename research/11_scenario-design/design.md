# Phase 11: Scenario-Based Research Design

status: completed
method: comparative scenario analysis with AI role variation

## Research Logic

This design evaluates not whether AI is broadly useful, but whether different levels of AI involvement produce better decisions under different conditions. The same decision problem is tested across multiple scenarios, and the AI role is varied from no AI to bounded automation.

## Scenario Structure

Each scenario should contain:

1. domain
2. business context
3. triggering condition
4. decision required
5. available data
6. AI role condition
7. expected benefits
8. expected risks
9. evaluation criteria

## Comparison Model

For each domain, compare:

- human-only decision
- AI assist
- AI recommend
- AI automate with guardrails

The comparison should test whether higher autonomy improves speed or consistency but worsens oversight, fairness, explainability, or safety.

## Domain Design

### Operations

The core question is whether AI should move from assist to controlled automation in repeated routing, scheduling, and demand decisions under both normal and disrupted conditions.

### Finance

The core question is whether AI should remain assist or tightly governed recommend when decisions affect access to credit, fraud monitoring, or risk classification, especially under adverse macro conditions.

### Healthcare

The core question is whether AI should remain assist or tightly governed recommend when patient safety, triage quality, or diagnostic reliability are exposed to uncertainty and edge cases.

## Evaluation Dimensions

### Performance

- speed
- consistency
- operational quality

### Governance

- explainability
- accountability
- auditability

### Harm

- fairness risk
- compliance risk
- safety risk

## Interpretation Rule

If higher AI autonomy improves operational performance but degrades explainability, fairness, or safety beyond acceptable limits, the preferred mode should remain assist or recommend rather than automation.

## Expected Contribution

This scenario design makes the study more defensible because it compares AI roles under structured conditions instead of relying only on general claims about domain suitability.
