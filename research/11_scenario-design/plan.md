# Phase 11: Scenario-Based Research Plan

status: active
goal: Redesign the study so AI is evaluated through alternative business scenarios rather than only through descriptive cross-domain comparison.

## Why This Phase

The current research shows that AI suitability varies by domain, risk, and governance burden. A scenario-based design is the next logical step because it allows the study to compare how AI changes decisions under different environmental conditions rather than only describing where AI is used.

## Core Research Shift

previous_focus: Which business domains are more or less suitable for AI-supported decision-making?
new_focus: How does the role of AI change decision quality under alternative business scenarios?

## Core Research Question

Under what conditions does AI improve decision quality across different business scenarios when it is used to assist, recommend, or automate decisions?

## Scope for First Scenario Study

The first scenario-based study should focus on three domains with strong contrast:

1. operations
2. finance
3. healthcare

## Why These Three

- operations provides a lower-risk and measurable decision environment
- finance provides a regulated and fairness-sensitive environment
- healthcare provides a safety-critical and evidence-intensive environment

## AI Role Conditions

Each scenario should compare four decision modes:

1. human-only
2. AI assist
3. AI recommend
4. AI automate with guardrails

## Outcome Measures

The study should evaluate:

1. decision speed
2. decision consistency
3. decision quality
4. explainability
5. risk exposure
6. fairness or compliance risk
7. required oversight burden

## Scenario Types

Each domain should include at least three scenario classes:

1. normal operating condition
2. stress or shock condition
3. edge case or failure condition

## Planned Deliverables

- scenario-based research design
- dataset shortlist
- reusable scenario template
- domain-specific scenario tables

## Immediate Next Step

Build the first scenario pack for operations, finance, and healthcare using public datasets and official regulatory context.
