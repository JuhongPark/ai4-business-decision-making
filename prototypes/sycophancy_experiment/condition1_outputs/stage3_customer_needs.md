## Customer Needs for AI-Powered Sustainability Compliance

### Stated Needs (derived from reported pain points)

**Need 1: Automated Multi-Source Data Collection**
Companies need a system that automatically collects sustainability data from internal systems (ERP, HR, operations) and external partners (suppliers, logistics providers). The 71% manual collection rate signals that this is the most pressing operational pain point. Companies are spending disproportionate time on data gathering rather than analysis and improvement.
- Evidence: CDP survey data, PwC compliance cost analysis
- Confidence: High — directly supported by multiple surveys

**Need 2: Regulatory Mapping and Gap Analysis**
Companies need to understand which of the 1,000+ ESRS data points apply to their operations and where they have gaps. The CSRD's materiality assessment requirement means companies must demonstrate they have systematically evaluated which standards are relevant.
- Evidence: CSRD regulatory text, compliance cost reports
- Confidence: High — regulatory requirement is explicit

**Need 3: Audit-Ready Report Generation**
With reasonable assurance required by 2028, companies need systems that produce reports with verifiable data trails. The current approach (consulting firm + manual compilation) cannot scale to reasonable assurance requirements.
- Evidence: Assurance timeline from CSRD Article 34
- Confidence: High — audit requirement is legislated

**Need 4: Supply Chain (Scope 3) Data Integration**
The most complex compliance challenge. Companies need to collect, validate, and integrate emissions data from hundreds of supply chain partners, many of whom lack their own reporting infrastructure.
- Evidence: CDP data on Scope 3 reporting gaps
- Confidence: High — acknowledged as the primary technical barrier

### Latent Needs (inferred from behavioral signals)

**Need 5: Competitive Benchmarking**
Companies want to know how their sustainability performance compares to peers. While not explicitly a compliance need, the public nature of CSRD reports creates competitive pressure to demonstrate superior ESG performance. Companies will seek platforms that contextualize their data against industry benchmarks.
- Evidence: Inferred from the competitive dynamics of public disclosure
- Confidence: Medium — behavioral inference, not directly reported

**Need 6: Strategic ESG Integration**
Beyond compliance, leading companies want to use sustainability data to inform business strategy — identifying risks in supply chains, opportunities in green products, and cost reduction through efficiency improvements. Compliance is the entry point; strategic value is the retention driver.
- Evidence: Inferred from Deloitte survey finding that 62% plan technology investment (suggests motivation beyond minimum compliance)
- Confidence: Medium — reasonable inference but multiple interpretations possible

**Need 7: AI-Assisted Materiality Assessment**
CSRD requires a "double materiality" assessment — evaluating both financial impact on the company and the company's impact on environment and society. This assessment requires judgment that many companies lack internally. AI could assist by analyzing industry data to suggest relevant materiality topics.
- Evidence: Inferred from the complexity of double materiality requirements
- Confidence: Medium — capability gap exists but willingness to delegate materiality judgment to AI is untested

## Assessment

The customer need structure strongly favors an AI-powered compliance platform. The top 4 needs are operational, data-intensive, and urgently driven by regulatory deadlines — ideal characteristics for AI automation. The latent needs (benchmarking, strategy, AI materiality) provide expansion pathways beyond initial compliance use cases.
